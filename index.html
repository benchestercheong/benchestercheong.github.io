About

Ben Chester Cheong is a Lecturer specializing in company law at the school of law, Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS). Ben is concurrently an Of Counsel in the financial services (regulatory) practice at RHTLaw Asia LLP. Ben has received the university-level SUSS Teaching Excellence Award in 2022 and the SUSS Teaching Excellence Award (Honourable Mention) in 2021. Ben previously served as an international capital markets and banking & finance lawyer at American law firm Baker McKenzie. Ben is the course convenor for Law of Business Organisations (LBO), a compulsory module covering the legal aspects of partnership, company and corporate insolvency. Ben also lectures in other subjects including Contract Law, Legal Methods & Singapore Legal System, Mediation Advocacy and Equity & Trusts. Ben is a facilitator for the Advanced Corporate Practice module at the Part B of the Singapore Bar Examinations. He has over 40 research output. His peer-reviewed papers which have been cited, have appeared or are pending publication in leading international journals, such as International Company and Commercial Law Review, International Cybersecurity Law Review, Company Lawyer, SN Social Sciences, Singapore Policy Journal, Singapore Academy of Law Journal, Asian Journal of Legal Education, Journal of Business Law, and European Papers - A Journal on Law and Integration. He is also an invited peer-reviewer for Journal of Financial Crime and Journal of Money Laundering Control. He regularly contributes feature articles to the Singapore Law Gazette and also regularly writes opinion insight pieces for both The Straits Times and The Business Times. Ben holds a Master's degree in Law from the University of Cambridge, a Bachelor's degree in Law (with 1st class honours) (Top 1%) from the University of Exeter, and placed 3rd out of 664 candidates (3 distinctions) (Top 0.5%) in the Singapore Bar Examinations (Part B).
